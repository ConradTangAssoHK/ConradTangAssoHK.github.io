Units in Cadastre


The unit 'acre' was used in the Demarcation District Survey in 1898 - 1905. The surveyed lot area was recorded in the Field Area Statement (FAS) then transcribed to the registered area in the Schedule.

However, the area recorded in the FAS and Schedule were rounded to the nearest 0.01 acre. Which means a latrine (lavatory/ toilet) with an area of 0.002 acre or a pigsty with area of 0.01 acre were booked 0.01 acre.

The Surveyors (Gunter's) chain

The surveyor's chain (or the Gunter's chain) A measuring equipment introduced in the 16th century by Edmund Gunter. This equipment was used for surveying and plotting parcels of land for legal purposes and transactions.

A Gunter's chain consists 100 consecutive links, each link with a length of 0.66 foot (or 7.92 inches/ 0.2012 meter). The links are marked into groups of 10 for reference.


Conversion between units

  • 1 yard = 0.9144 meter
  • 1 foot = 0.9144 / 3 = 0.3048 meter
  • 1 inch = 0.3048 / 12 = 0.0254 meter

  • 1 chain = 66 feet = 20.1168 meters

  • 1 meter ≈ 1.0936 yard
  • 1 meter ≈ 3.2808 feet

Conversion between units

1 acre = 1 chain × 1 furlong
= 66 feet × 660 feet
= 43,560 sq. feet
or in metric unit:
= (66 × 0.3048) × (660 × 0.3048)
≈ 4048.86 sq. meters

Note: 0.01 acre in Chinese is also called "一分地".


An acre equals to 1 chain × 1 furlong (sometimes written as 2 chains × 5 furlongs as shown in figure 1), which is in rectangular shape when drawn. If it is drawn in square, the dimension of an acre will be approximately 63.6149 × 63.6149 ≈ 4046.86 sq. meters. (Figure 2)




To convert an area in acre unit between sq. meters, we can:

Example: a parcel of land of 0.46 acre

1 acre ≈ 4048.86 sq. meters
0.46 acre ≈ 0.46 × 4048.86 ≈ 1861.56 sq. meters
Similarly, 0.46 acre = 0.46 × 43,560 = 20,037 sq. feet

Conversely, 1 sq. meter ≈ 1/4046.86 ≈ 2.4711 × 10-4 acre
Therefore, 1861.56 sq. meters ≈ 1861.56 × 2.4711 × 10-4 ≈ 0.46 acre
