Computerized Land Information System (CLIS)


In 1989, the Survey & Mapping Office of the Lands Department began to set up its Land Information System using ARC/INFO as the basic application software. This Computerized Land Information System (CLIS) aims to convert the existing 1:1000 survey sheet mapping information into digital base information. The conversion work is being carried out by Bureau Service and it is completed in 1996. The digital map features are separately coded thereby enabling selective retrieval and display is use definable colors and symbols.

The System initially contains some of the most commonly used land data, the maintenance of which requires considerable manpower. This includes:

  • Basic Mapping at scale 1:1000 covering the developed areas in the territory.
  • Basic Mapping at scale 1:1000 covering the developed areas in the territory.
  • Land Records Plans showing the locations of all land lots and land disposal proposals.
  • The System comprises three main application sub-system, viz. the Basic Mapping System (BMS), Cadastral Information System (CIS) and the Geographic Information Retrieval System (GIRS).
  • There are 15 layers of geographical features maintained in the map library of the BMS.
  • The CIS maintains the land boundary record database, facilities the preparation of cadastral plans and answers enquires on land status. There are many types of land disposal and different standard for recording their positions. Temporary land parcels sometimes overlap due to the different development schedules. To record all different types of land parcel boundaries to meet land administration need, a total of 33 data layers have been created in the CIS map library.
  • The GIRS supports the BMS and CIS. It has been developed to facilitate access to both the graphic and textual information of an area via a number of identifier, such as the house address, building name, street intersection, lot number, planning zone unit and geographic coordinates. Generally, the textual attributes stored with some of the graphic data in the CIS data layers. Besides using the textual data of the CIS data layers can be used as identifiers for information retrieval. However, 3 special data layers derived from the BMS data layers are also maintained in the GIRS for spatial enquires.
  • Many house lots have been left as block and not annotated in the LIP. It must be emphasis that these are all internal records and are not intended to be the sole definitive records of boundary definition. Field verifications need to be carried out for land administration actions of individual house lots.

The current Lot Index Plans (LIP) are the result of repeated correlation and has been employed as the basis of private land boundaries for all land administration actions including resumption in the NT.

Since searching of all necessary records for re-establishment of boundary has been painstaking and incomplete, District Survey Offices (DSOs) have created a retrieval system for organizing the records. Different DSO will have different retrieval system. Let's use District Survey Office/North (DSO/N) as an example


Survey Records Retrieval System (SRRS)

The Survey Records Retrieval System (SRRS), which can retrieve all relevant records of any lots/subdivisions within a matter of seconds has been successfully implemented in the DSO/N since August 2001.


Objectives

To store all the scanned image of the land boundary records in DSO/N and to perform comprehensive search of a lot/subdivision of all available records in the office. The system is executed in three different stages:

  1. Searching all relevant records of a particular land parcel
  2. Display/Print/Part-print images at reduced/enlarged scales
  3. Perform the process of correlation of boundary on screen

System Configuration

The system adopted Visual Basic as the programming language and runs on PC of PIII or above with Windows 98/NT/ME/XP as the operating system. It also makes use of free commercial software such as "ER Viewer" and "Jpegger" for viewing scanned images. With the use of "Photoshop", correlation of DD Sheet/Survey Sheet with corresponding aerial photo, records sheet or/and survey sheets can be done on the computer within seconds.

Application

1. Searching

The system provides five methods of searching to users to retrieve information of a particular lot/subdivision:

  • Full Search
  • Lot Number only
  • Co-ordinates only
  • Plan/Photo/Computation Folder/Work File/Sheet Number
  • Location











2. Approximate Location of Land Parcel

One of the most useful applications is to show the approximate geographical location of a land parcel on the map base. This particular function can be performed on any image associated with geographical reference such as DD Sheet, Aerial Photo, Survey Sheet, etc.

For aerial photo, the geographical reference is computed from the digitized principal points of the photo index. Approximate location of the land parcel can be displayed on screen, which greatly facilitates the identification of land parcel. This application has greatly relieved the painful and tedious process of identifying photo details and scaling from a map.






3. Production of Lot Index Plan

Standard A4 and A3 LIP frames have been created in the System. LIP can be produced within 10 minutes after request by utilizing the SRRS and Photoshop clipping function. Turnaround time for the production of LIP can now be significantly reduced and consequently shortened the waiting time of customer.


4. Correlation of Boundary

With the installation of Photoshop software, multiple images can be viewed on screen. Images can be rotated, translated, reduced or enlarged to appropriate scale. The manual process of correlation can now be easily done on computer screen. Turnaround time, which previously took weeks using reprographic process is now dramatically shortened to around 15 minutes.


5. Production of Setting Out Plan

Setting-out plan of the Building License can readily be produced based on the image of the license plan by means of the tools available in the Photoshop. Again, the setting-out plan can be produced within minutes.






6. Viewing Current CIS/BMS

By the end of each working day, image files of modified map sheets of the CIS are created, then transferred to the SRRS server and replaced obsolete image files. Therefore, SRRS users can also view current BMS information. The advantage of viewing CIS/BMS sheet by means of the SRRS is that images can be retrieved almost instantly on screen.


7. Identify the Location of Computer Folder and Work Files

In DSO/N, movements of the Correspondence Files, Computation Folders and Working Files are tracked by the Bar-Coding File Management System (BCFMS) developed by the Management Service Agency. Special written programs aimed at synchronizing two databases of the BCFMS and the SRRS have been developed. Thus, the SRRS can now identify the location of the Computation Folders and Work Files more conveniently.
