Professional Institutions


Cadastral survey is a major realm for the land surveying professionals in Hong Kong. A message from Hong Kong Institute of Surveyors (HKIS) stated that "It is always advisable for landowners and developers to ascertain the boundaries and area entitlements of their properties by land boundary surveys prior to any land development."


Authorized Land Surveyors

Authorized Land Surveyors (ALS) are private land surveyors registered under the Land Survey Ordinance (Cap 473). The duties of Authorized Land Surveyors include the approval of Land Boundary Plans for subdivision purpose and other surveying works. He or She will be personally liable for the accuracy and completeness of the land boundary plans certified by him or her and be responsible for damages suffered by any person as a result of any inaccuracy and incompleteness of any land boundary plans. Registered Professional Surveyors (RPS)

In July 1991, the Surveyors Registration Ordinance (Cap 417) was enacted to provide for the registration of professional surveyors, the recognition of six divisions within the profession and disciplinary control of the RPS. These six professional surveyor divisions are:


Building Surveyor

RPS (BS)

General Practice Surveyor

RPS (GP)

Land Surveyor

RPS (LS)

Planning and Development Surveyor

RPS (PD)

Property and Facility Management Surveyor

RPS (PFM)

Quantity Surveyor

RPS (QS)



Hong Kong Institute of Surveyors (HKIS)

http://www.hkis.org.hk/en/

The Hong Kong Institute of Surveyors (HKIS) is one of the professional organization representing the surveying profession in Hong Kong. The tasks for the Institute includes setting standards for professional services and performances, establishing codes of ethics, determining requirements for professional surveyors admission, and encouraging members to upgrade skills through continuing professional development. In January 1990, The Hong Kong Institute of Surveyors Ordinance (Cap 1148) was enacted. This ordinance is to make provision for the incorporation of the HKIS. Currently, the Institute has seven divisions:

  • Building Surveying Division
  • General Practice Division
  • Land Surveying Division
  • Planning and Development Division
  • Property and Facility Management Division
  • Quantity Surveying Division
  • Young Surveyors Division

Royal Institution of Chartered Surveyors (RICS)

http://www.rics.org/hk/

The Royal Institution of Chartered Surveyors (RICS) is a professional organization representing the surveying profession in Hong Kong. The tasks for the organization includes the promotions on the professional qualifications and standards in the development and management on the specialisms in land, real estate, construction and infrastructure.



The Hong Kong Institute of Land Surveyors (HKILS) (Amalgamated)

Since there was a group of both local and expatriate professional land surveyors decided to provide a professional forum for all land surveyors in Hong Kong, The Hong Kong Institute of Land Surveyors (HKILS) was then established in 1976. This Institution made a contribution to land survey that suggested the introduction of legal measures to improve the system in Hong Kong in 1977, before that Hong Kong was a place without land surveying ordinance or any relevant ordinance.

In 1984, HKILS was formally recognized by the government as the only professional body representing the professional land surveyors. However, due to the establishment of Hong Kong Institute of Surveyors (HKIS) which recognized a qualification for appointment to the relevant grades, HKILS is being amalgamated with HKIS in 1991.
